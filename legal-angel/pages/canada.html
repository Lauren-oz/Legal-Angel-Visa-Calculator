Canada
See your visa options below

Working holiday work permit

Australians aged 18 – 35 can live and work in Canada for up to 2 years with an International Experience Canada (IEC) Working Holiday work permit.

There are a number of steps to successfully get this visa, including being placed into a pool of IEC candidates and only being able to proceed to apply by invitation.

Work permit

You may receive a temporary work permit if:

  1. you receive an offer of employment from a Canadian company
  2. the Canadian company who has offered to employ you, has applied to Human Resources Development Canada to prove that your position will benefit the Canadian Labour Market and is no threat to Canadian citizens employment opportunities, and
  3. if the Canadian company successfully applies to Citizenship and Immigration Canada for the work permit.
An employer-specific work permit is not transferable to other employers, meaning you will not be able to stay and work in Canada if your employment is terminated.

However, you can apply to change your Canadian immigration status when you are in Canada.

You may also be able to get an open work permit, which would allow you to work for any Canadian employer, but this is only available in specific situations, as listed on the Canada Government website.

Study permit

Australians who are planning to study at a designated learning institution in Canada can apply for a study permit before they arrive in Canada.

A study permit is not a visa and the Canadian Government will also need to issue you a visitor visa or an electronic travel authorisation (eTA) along with your study permit.

A study permit will allow you to stay in Canada for the length of your studies, plus an extra 90 days.

Temporary resident visa

A temporary resident visa will allow you to stay in Canada for up to 6 months but does not allow you to work in Canada.

Family sponsorship

Canadian citizens who are at least 18 years old can sponsor spouses, children, grandchildren and other relatives to come to Canada.

The Canadian citizen will also need to prove that they can support you financially and that you will not need social assistance from the Canadian Government.

Canadian citizenship

To be eligible to become a Canadian citizen, you must have lived in Canada for 3 out of the last 5 years, be a permanent resident, have paid taxes, passed a test on your rights, responsibilities and knowledge of Canada and prove your language skills.
