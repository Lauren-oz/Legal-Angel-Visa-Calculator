E-3 working visa

The E-3 working visa is a special visa just for Australian citizens who have received a job offer from a US sponsoring company and who are going to the US to work in a speciality occupation.

A specialty occupation is an occupation that requires:

  • a theoretical and practical application of a body of specialized knowledge; and
  • the attainment of a bachelor’s or higher degree in the specific specialty (or its equivalent) as a minimum for entry into the occupation in the United States.
To receive an E-3 visa you must go through the application process, including completing forms, paying a fee and booking and attending an in person interview with a consular officer.

If you receive an E-3 visa, your spouse and children, even if they are not Australian citizens, will be able to apply for visas to accompany you to the US and to work in the US.

It is important to note that the E-3 visa has a limit of around 10,500 Australians who can receive the visa each year and that the visa allows Australians to work in the US for 2 years but can be renewed indefinitely.

If you are a skilled tradesperson, for example with experience in plumbing, electrical and carpentry work, the E-3 visa is not right for you because you normally would not need a bachelor’s degree to perform this work.

Student visas F or M

To study in the US, you must be accepted into a Student and Exchange Visitor Program (SEVP) approved school and then go through the process to pay for and apply for a student visa F or M.

Your spouse and children may be able to live with you whilst you study in the US but they must also go through an application process to receive a visa.

For short periods of recreational study, which is not for credit towards a degree or academic certificate, Australians should investigate the ESTA (discussed below).

Exchange visitor visa J

To go on exchange in the US, you must apply for and be accepted into an exchange program through a designated US sponsoring organisation in the US.

Once accepted, you will need to complete the steps to get an Exchange Visitor Visa J.

Your spouse and children may be able to live with you whilst you complete your exchange program in the US but they must also go through an application process to receive a visa.

Short visits with the Electronic System for Travel Authorization (ESTA)

Australian citizens travelling to the USA must apply for an ESTA at least 72 hours before departing for the US.

The ESTA allows Australian citizens to visit the US for tourism, business, transit, medical and short study purposes without a visa for up to 90 days per visit.

The ESTA can be used for multiple entries to the US and has 2 years of validity or date of passport expiry, whichever is sooner.

Immigrant visa

Australians who are the spouse, fiancé, parent, step-parent, child or step-child under the age of 21 can go through the process of applying for an Immigrant Visa to move to the US.

Play the green card lottery

Australians can apply for the Diversity Visa Program, also known as the Green Card Lottery or DV Lottery.

A limited number of visas, around 55,000, are available each fiscal year through the Diversity Visa Program.

Make sure to apply when the program opens back up.

Look into US citizenship

To become a US citizen, you must have been born in the US or have parents who were US citizens at the time of your birth.

Alternatively you must be at least 18 years old, establish continuous and lawful residency in the US for 5 years (or 3 years if you are married to a US citizen), apply for naturalisation, take a test and attend a citizenship ceremony and take an oath of allegiance to the US.
