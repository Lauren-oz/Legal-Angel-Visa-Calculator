Youth mobility scheme visa (tier 5)

Australians who are aged 18 – 30 and have enough savings can apply for a Youth Mobility Scheme Visa.

The Youth Mobility Scheme Visa entitles Aussies to live and work in the UK for up to 2 years.

Note that Australians who have turned 30 can apply up until their 31st birthday - making it possible to arrive in the UK aged 31 and stay for 2 years. This has been an area of confusion for friends so I want to make this very clear!

General work visa (Tier 2)

If you are too old for the Youth Mobility Scheme Visa or would like to stay in the UK after your Youth Mobility Scheme Visa has expired, you can go down the sponsorship route, which will allow you to live and work in the UK for up to 5 years.

Sponsorship is only available to UK businesses who are licenced sponsors and if your role is for a “shortage occupation”.

To sponsor you, your UK sponsoring company will need to complete paper work, advertise your role to other people and pay a fee to the UK Government in order to sponsor you.

Let’s discuss some of the key risks associate with this visa:

  1. If you are on a Youth Mobility Scheme Visa and hoping to stay longer in the UK longer through sponsorship, beware that a UK company may falsely or naively claim they can sponsor you so they get you to work for them and then not actually end up sponsoring you at the end of your visa.
  2. It is not uncommon for UK employers to try to get the Australian employee to pay for their own sponsorship. To avoid a nasty surprise, ask your employer if they expect you to pay.

General student visa (Tier 4)

A General Student Visa is available for people aged 16 and over, who have enough money to support themselves and who have been offered a place in the course of a UK licensed tier 4 sponsor.

If you have a General Student Visa you can stay in the UK for the amount of time that it takes to complete your course and there are restrictions placed on your UK working rights.

Global talent visa

With a Global Talent Visa you could stay and work in the UK anywhere from 1 – 5 years.

To receive a Global Talent Visa, you must work in a qualifying field and have been endorsed as a recognised leader, an emerging leader or under the UK Research and Innovation endorsed funder option.

UK ancestry visa

A UK Ancestry Visa would allow you to live and work in the UK for up to 5 years if at least one of your grandparents was born in the UK and you can prove it.

Family visa (partner or spouse)

If you are married to or in a de-facto relationship with a UK citizen, you may be able to apply for a Family Visa.

British or EU citizenship

If one of your parents was born in the UK or if you were born in the UK, you can register to become a British Citizen and then apply for a British passport.

You may also become a British citizen if you have lived in the UK for 3 years and marry a British citizen or have settled status under the EU Settlement Scheme.

You should also investigate whether you could get a European Union passport, as this will allow you to live and work in the UK for as long as the UK remains part of the European Union.

Standard visitor visa

A Visitor Visa would allow you to stay but not work in the UK for up to 6 months, depending on your circumstances.
